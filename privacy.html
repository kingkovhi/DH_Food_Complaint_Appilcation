Privacy Policy – Campus Food Voice

Effective Date: 2025-06-16

This Privacy Policy explains how Campus Food Voice (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information when you use our web application. We are committed to respecting your privacy and handling your data transparently.

1. Data we collect

When you register and use the app, we collect the following information:

  • Full name – to identify you to hall staff (not shown publicly).
  • Student number – for verification and hall association.
  • Email address – used for login, account recovery, and verification.
  • Dining hall assignment – to route your feedback to the correct hall admin.
  • Submissions – content of your complaints, concerns, or compliments, including title, description, and any attached replies.
  • Timestamps – when you submit, edit, or delete posts.

We do not collect sensitive data such as payment information, location, or biometrics.

2. How we use your data

  • To manage your feedback and route it to the appropriate hall administrator.
  • To enable you to track the status of your submissions.
  • To allow staff to respond and resolve issues.
  • To send you password reset or email verification messages.
  • To improve our services and analyse usage patterns (aggregated, not identifying individual users).

3. Who has access to your data

  • Hall administrators (staff assigned to your hall) can see your name, student number, and the full content of submissions from their hall. They cannot see submissions from other halls.
  • Main administrators (e.g., catering management, SRC) can see all submissions across all halls.
  • Your submissions are displayed in the Community Feed without your name or student number – they are anonymous to other students.
  • We do not share your data with third parties for marketing or any other purposes.

4. Data storage and security

All data is stored securely in Firebase Firestore and Firebase Authentication, both provided by Google. Data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Access is controlled by Firebase Security Rules that restrict read/write operations to authenticated users with appropriate roles.

🔒 Security measures: We enforce email verification, rate limiting, and require re-authentication for sensitive actions (like account deletion).

5. Your rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: You can view your profile and all your submissions in the “My Submissions” tab.
  • Correction: You can edit your submissions or update your profile (name, student number, hall) – contact us for profile changes.
  • Deletion: You can permanently delete your entire account and all associated data using the “Delete Account” button in the app. This removes your user profile, all your submissions, and your authentication account.
  • Data portability: You can request a copy of your data by contacting us (see section 9).

6. Data retention

We retain your data as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, all your submissions and profile data are removed from our database within a few seconds. If you stop using the app without deleting your account, we may retain your data for up to 12 months after your last login, after which we may anonymise or delete it.

7. Cookies and tracking

We do not use cookies or any third‑party tracking tools. The app uses only essential local storage (e.g., user session data) for functionality.

8. Children’s privacy

Our service is intended for university students who are 18 years or older. We do not knowingly collect data from minors.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy occasionally. The latest version will always be available at this URL. If we make significant changes, we will notify users via email or a notice in the app.

10. Contact us

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding your privacy, please contact:

  • Email: privacy@campusfoodvoice.com
  • SRC: Contact your hall SRC representative

This privacy policy complies with the principles of the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) and GDPR.
